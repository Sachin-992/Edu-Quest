# EDUCORE School Identity & Password Policy
## Parent & Guardian Information Notice

---

## 📋 Purpose of This Document

This document explains how our school's digital system manages student and parent login credentials. Our approach prioritizes **security**, **simplicity**, and **accountability** while protecting your child's privacy.

---

## 🔐 How Logins Work

### Email is Your Username
Every user (student, parent, teacher, administrator) logs in using their **email address** as their username. This makes it easy to remember and impossible to confuse with someone else's account.

### No Self-Registration
For security, **no one can create their own account**. All accounts are created by school administrators. This ensures:
- Only authorized individuals have access
- We know exactly who is in our system
- Fake accounts cannot be created

---

## 🎓 Student Passwords

### Why Date of Birth?
Your child's password is their **date of birth** in the format **DDMMYYYY**.

**Example:** If your child was born on March 15, 2010, their password is `15032010`

### Why This Approach?
1. **Easy to Remember**: Both you and your child know this date
2. **Hard to Guess**: Only family members know the exact date
3. **No Forgotten Passwords**: You'll never need to reset it
4. **Age-Appropriate**: Young students can't be expected to remember complex passwords

### Can Students Change Their Password?
**No.** Student passwords are permanently locked to their date of birth. This is intentional because:
- Students may share passwords with friends if allowed to change them
- It prevents bullying scenarios where someone changes another student's password
- Parents always know their child's password for supervision

---

## 👨‍👩‍👧 Parent Passwords

### Your Password = Your Child's Date of Birth
As a parent, your password is **your child's date of birth**, NOT your own.

**Example:** If your child was born on March 15, 2010, YOUR password is `15032010`

### Why?
1. **One Thing to Remember**: You already know your child's birthday
2. **Family Connection**: Both you and your child use the same "key"
3. **Supervised Access**: If your child knows their password, you automatically know yours

### Can Parents Change Their Password?
**No.** Like student passwords, parent passwords are locked. This ensures:
- Consistency and predictability
- No "I forgot my password" situations
- Easy re-access if you forget (just remember your child's birthday)

### What If I Have Multiple Children?
Your password is linked to one child (usually the eldest or first-enrolled). Your account will have access to all your children's information.

---

## 👨‍🏫 Teacher Passwords

### Initial Password
Teachers receive their **own date of birth** as their initial password.

### Must Change on First Login
Unlike students and parents, teachers are **required to change their password** after their first login. This is because:
- Teachers have access to sensitive student data
- They are adults who can manage secure passwords
- Unique passwords add an extra layer of protection

### Teachers Can Reset Student Passwords
If a student is locked out, their teacher can reset the password back to the date of birth. This provides:
- Quick support without contacting the office
- Teacher accountability (all resets are logged)

---

## 🔒 How We Protect Privacy

### Date of Birth is Never Visible
Your date of birth is **encrypted** (scrambled) in our database. Even our technical staff cannot see it directly. When displayed, it appears as `**/**/2010` (year only).

### Every Action is Logged
Our system keeps a secure record of:
- Who logged in and when
- Any password resets
- Who made changes to accounts
- Failed login attempts

This audit trail:
- Helps us investigate any issues
- Deters misuse
- Provides evidence if needed

### Database-Level Security
All security rules are enforced by the database itself, not just the website. This means:
- Even if our website had a bug, unauthorized access would still be blocked
- Rules cannot be bypassed
- Protection works 24/7 automatically

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: What if someone knows my child's birthday?
**A:** While birthdays aren't secret, accessing the account also requires knowing the email address. Unauthorized access attempts are logged and flagged. If you're concerned, please contact the school.

### Q: Can anyone see my child's password?
**A:** No. The password is stored in encrypted form. Even administrators cannot view it—they can only reset it.

### Q: What happens if I enter the wrong password?
**A:** Failed login attempts are recorded. After multiple failures, the account may be temporarily locked for security.

### Q: Who can create accounts?
**A:** Only school administrators with verified access can create accounts. Teachers cannot create accounts; they can only assist with password resets for their own students.

### Q: Is my data shared with anyone?
**A:** Your login information is never shared outside the school system. We do not sell or distribute personal data.
